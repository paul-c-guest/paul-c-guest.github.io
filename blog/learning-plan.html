24 June 2023

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Long term, I'd like to be settled in a team of excellent people that I can have a laugh with. It's so much easier to do hard work with people when you're all in it together. I feel that everything's been coming together to this career decision (software development). It's taken many years and the time is now.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I can be a focussed investigator if something interesting strikes me as requiring some sudden and deep research. So, rather than creating endless rabbit-holes to disappear down, I can deploy this as a well-practiced strategy - rapid research and digestion to get just the information I need and move on.

I've never really stopped learning! I like learning something new every day and am able to take on new concepts and approaches quickly. I desire knowledge and often choose to spend spare time in some kind of learning mode.

I could be prone to easy distraction if I'm not really invested in what I'm learning.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I joined Dev Academy in part because I wanted the challenge of learning about web software in a team environment. It is very important to me that I gain these kinds of experiences; developing something that is so big that no single person can keep track of it all, requiring trust in your colleagues, empathy when something goes wrong, the human skills to navigate all this and still keep the project going.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I will expect myself to 'show up to work' in the ordinary working hours of the week. Extra hours will likely spill over to times after dinner and mornings in the weekend. Most of my workload should therefore be taken care of by treating this as full-time employment — I should be present.

I will assume that other learners are available for communication during similar 'working hours' unless I know otherwise; and will limit bothering them with anything if it seems like I wouldn't want to be bothered at that hour either.

When things are building up and getting on top of me, I won't keep digging that hole! I'll go for a walk around the block, or remove myself from the task, and tell someone that I'm doing so, and for what reason.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

When I recognise myself going round in circles for a bit, I'll reach out to others in my homegroup, or someone in particular if I know they'll be able to help; and explain my problem and what I've tried, where I think it's going wrong etc. I'll give enough time for suitable help before escalating if necessary to the facilitators — but I might also just go straight to them if it seems like I'll get quicker help.

It'll be important to not keep digging my own hole in secret. I should call for help early, since someone else might have a simple answer or fresh eyes for the problem.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect that the facilitators will be helpful and respectful of my knowledge gaps, as I will be doing the same for other learners around me. I do not expect they'll just give me the answers, but I trust that they will be able to put me on the right track.

I expect to be pushed by my facilitators, to be challenged to continually raise my standards.
