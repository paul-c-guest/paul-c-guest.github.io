Te Whare Tapa Whā

dimensions of wellbeing

3 July 2023

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model of the four dimensions of wellbeing, developed by Tā Mason Durie in 1984 to provide a Māori perspective on health. Here are most of the things I do to keep my four walls standing:

Taha Tinana

I look after my physical wellbeing by walking, running, cycling, avoiding takeaway food, not eating meat, cooking for myself, not using soap, and not using drugs.

Taha Whānau

I look after my wellbeing by connecting with my friends for a chat or a laugh, and seeking out people that I can be myself around.

Taha Hinengaro

I look after my mental & emotional wellbeing by talking openly with someone special; but also by letting my mind drift while running.

Taha Wairua

Although I am not a spiritual person, I look after this aspect of my wellbeing by being a principled person; acting honestly and trusting in others. I look up to the night sky in humility to remind myself of my own insignificance.

Whenua

I connect with the land often. I greet the native birds as they pass; I acknowledge and respect the age of the native forest. I enjoy being outside in the rain; I enjoy floating in the surf.
